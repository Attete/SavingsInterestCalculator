The purpose of this calculator is to determine the final balance of a fixed term deposit in order to provide an illustration as required by banking regulations. See end of guide for specific regulation.

Getting Started:

The calculator is optimised for the Chrome web browser and should be accessed via Chrome whenever possible. The user guide requires a base knowledge of fixed rate savings products and their features.

Input Parameters:


There are 4 main input parameters in the Setup section of the tool. All are mandatory in order for the calculation to work.

Product Launch Date:b This is the date the product is intended to be put on sale, and therefore is the first day interest is to be calculated from.
Product End Date: This is the final day of the fixed term, when the product will mature and the final amount of interest will be paid.
Product Rate: The rate the customer receives. This will calculate as a percentage so to enter a rate of 1.55% simply type 1.55
Deposit Value: The initial deposit that the illustration is to be based on.


Interest Capitalisation Dates:

After entering the main product details, it is required to enter the dates that interest will be capitalised onto the account. These will generally be on the anniversaries of the product end date. These dates must be entered in the ascending order (i.e. the order in which they will be paid. The final capitalisation date must be equal to the product end date. The calculator allow up to 4 capitalisation dates, however if not required can be ignored. To see the results, press the large green Calculate button.

Outputs:

After pressing the calculate button, the next screen will populate from the results of the calculation.
The Setup section will provide confirmation of input parameters, but will also calculate a Monthly Rate. This confirms the rate payable to the customer if they choose the Monthly Interest version of the product.

Setup



The Calculations section shows the exact workings of the calculation for third parties to be able to verify the calculation.
Note: Any unused capitalisation date will display as 01/01/1900, however will not be used in the calculation.



The Summary section then confirms the final figures to be used in the illustration. The illustration is based exactly the on parameters input into the calculator.



Next Steps:

Pressing the Go Back button will take you back to the input sheet. In the first instance all the previously inputted data will be retained, to allow for simple corrections if a parameter was entered incorrectly. Pressing Reset All will clear the calculator for a fresh entry.
